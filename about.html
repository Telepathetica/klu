About This Place

A little cupboard of creative tech experiments

Stuff That Works is a casual collection of things I’ve built, designed, half-finished, accidentally fixed, or just got curious about. I’m not a guru, an influencer, or a lifestyle brand. I’m just good at making things work, eventually—sometimes the hard way.

I’m a generalist: websites, SEO, databases, spreadsheets, animations, creative projects. If you’ve ever thought “there must be a clever way to do this,” that’s usually where I show up. Quietly. With coffee.

Why this site exists

I wanted somewhere to log my projects, experiments, and side quests—especially the ones that didn’t quite fit into any professional box. It’s also a place I can point people to when they ask, “What kind of things do you do?”

It’s not a sales page. But if you like the way I think or want help with a creative, useful, slightly weird project—I might be up for it. Especially if it doesn’t involve too many meetings.

Things I enjoy

  • Designing and building websites that don’t break
  • Making animations that are slightly too ambitious
  • Helping small businesses make sense of tech and marketing
  • Playing with data, logic, and mild automation
  • Exploring ideas that might be useless but fun

Say hello

If you’re working on something and think I could help, drop me a line. I don’t always take on projects, but I do like interesting conversations.
